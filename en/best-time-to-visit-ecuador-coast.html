The best time to visit Ecuador's coast

Updated July 2026 · By the Sandpiper Villas team, Ayampe

Ecuador's coast is a year-round destination, but each season has its character. The good news: sitting right on the equator, temperatures stay warm all year. The difference is in the sun, the sea and — yes — the whales.

Sun and beach season: December to May

It's the high season for a reason: clear skies, warmer water and the best beach days. It also brings the year's best waves for surfing. If guaranteed sun and warm water are your priority, aim for these months (with New Year's and Easter as peak-demand windows).

Whale season: June to September

The sky turns greyer (the local "garúa" mist), but the sea comes alive: thousands of humpback whales arrive to breed off the Manabí coast. It's the best time for whale watching and usually brings lower rates. Surf stays active.

The secret months: October and November

The season transition. The town is quiet, skies begin to clear and rates are friendly. For anyone after Ayampe at its most peaceful, it's an ideal window — and exactly when Sandpiper Villas opens its doors.

Quick summary

  • For sun and beach: December–May
  • For whales: June–September
  • For surf: year-round, best Dec–May
  • For quiet and value: October–November

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time for the beach in Ecuador?

December to May: clear skies, warm water and the best sun and surf days on the coast.

When can you see whales on Ecuador's coast?

June to September, when humpback whales arrive to breed off Manabí. July and August are the peak months.

Does it rain a lot on Ecuador's coast?

The coast is dry much of the year. June to September brings the "garúa" (overcast skies and light drizzle) rather than heavy rain. The sunniest season is December–May.

When is Ayampe least crowded?

October and November are the quietest months, with pleasant transition weather and lower rates.
